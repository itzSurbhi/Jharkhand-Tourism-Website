Culture & Heritage

Experience the rich tribal traditions and cultural heritage of Jharkhand

Tribal Traditions

Jharkhand is home to over 30 tribal communities, each with unique customs, languages, and traditions. The state's cultural identity is deeply rooted in its tribal heritage, with festivals like Sarhul and Karma celebrating the harmony between humans and nature.

32 Tribal Communities

Rich diversity of indigenous cultures

Folk Arts

Traditional Handicrafts

Discover the authentic tribal crafts and artisan traditions of Jharkhand

Dokra Art

Ancient bell metal craft using lost-wax technique, practiced for over 4000 years. Creates beautiful tribal figurines and decorative items.

Copper Bronze Zinc

Bamboo Crafts

Sustainable bamboo products made by tribal communities using natural bamboo from local forests for eco-friendly living.

Natural Bamboo Eco-friendly Handmade

Tribal Jewelry

Traditional ornaments with cultural significance including Hansuli necklaces, indicating social status and tribal identity.

Silver Brass Beads

Kohvar & Sohrai

Traditional wall paintings by tribal women using natural pigments, celebrating marriage, fertility, and harvest seasons.

Festivals & Traditions

Experience the vibrant festivals and musical traditions of Jharkhand's tribal communities

Sarhul

Spring festival celebrating nature, worship of sal tree flowers. Marks arrival of spring and brotherhood with nature.

Spring Season (March-April)

Karma

Harvest festival worshipping the Karma tree for prosperity. Celebrates community bonding and agricultural traditions.

Harvest Season (August-September)

Chhath Puja

Sun worship festival with fasting and river rituals. Celebrates devotion to Sun God and family unity.

Winter Season (October-November)

Traditional Music & Dance

Chhau Dance

Semi-classical dance with tribal and folk traditions using masks for storytelling

Jhumair

Popular folk dance performed during harvest and festivals for community celebration

Banam

Single-string violin made from coconut shell and bamboo for solo performances

Mandar

Clay drum covered with goat skin, played with hands during folk songs and dances

Plan Your Visit to Jharkhand

Essential information for an unforgettable journey to the Land of Forests

Best Time to Visit

October to March

Winter season offers pleasant weather ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Temperature ranges from 15°C to 25°C.

Avoid monsoon season (July-September) due to heavy rainfall

How to Reach Jharkhand

By Air:
  • Birsa Munda Airport, Ranchi
  • Sonari Airport, Jamshedpur
  • Connected to Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata
By Rail:
  • Major stations: Ranchi, Jamshedpur, Dhanbad
  • Direct trains from major cities
By Road:
  • NH-33 connects Ranchi to Kolkata
  • State and private buses available

Travel Tips & Guidelines

Carry valid photo ID for hotel check-ins and travel
Book forest permits in advance for national parks
Respect tribal customs and photography restrictions
Carry sufficient cash as ATMs may be limited in rural areas
Pack comfortable walking shoes for trekking and sightseeing
Learn basic Hindi or local phrases for better communication
